Hello again, Lemon here.

I feel like I have a weird problem with creativity. As I've said before, I have a very "logic-based" brain. It's as if I think in direct answers. I don't care for personal interpretation. This kind of makes me think very directly though. I am not very creative. Trust me. When it comes to stuff that does not affect me in a major way, I will have issues deciding from personal preference. Ask me what I would like to eat and I would not be able to decide until I look in the fridge and see what I have. I can stare at a piece of paper and think to my self, "I want to draw a house" and it will take me a moment to figure out how to even draw a basic ass rectangle with a triangle on top. I just don't really picture much in my head.

You may have heard of something called Aphantasia. It's a condition where an individual cannot picture stuff in their mind. I don't have this condition but I would think that it ties into my limited creativity. You may have also heard what I am about to say but, if you haven't, hear this: Close your eyes, or not, and Picture an apple. Just an apple. It can be anywhere you want, any color, anything you want. What do you see? Do you see fine details? Stripes? Is it moist with water drops? Does it have a stem? Is it colorful? I'll tell you what I see. It's very hard to describe as I can't see it directly but I can sort of image it without color. If I try to hard to picture it, I can't see it but I also can. It's a very basic black and white apple with almost no detail. I'm not sure if it's always been this way but I don't really remember it being any other way. I find it fascinating how humans can simply just imagine stuff differently from one another. I know that sounds dumb but imagine you can picture an entirely different world in your head, however some people literally cannot see anything in their mind. Some people don't even have internal monologues which is also very interesting.
