Motivation Back

Aug 23, 2023

Hi, Lemon here.

I like writing code and programming. I really do. I feel like it ties into my "logically configured" mind. I love the challenge it can offer and the critical thinking I need to come up with solutions that aren't completely awful and make some sense. I'm definitely not that skilled when it comes to more advanced languages and things; Python and Lua are about the most complicated I've gone, but I love it and when I get into something interesting, I can really get attached. Hours will fly by and I will feel like I've made something fascinating.
Recently, I started my PoEPyAutopot (Path of Exile Python Autopot) project. As the name would imply, it's a very configurable auto-flask program for Path of Exile. Without getting too deep into the logistics, it scans your life and/or mana and, once low enough, will use flasks as configured. It works pretty dang well and I'm very proud with what I've done but I got distracted with making this website for a couple days and, once I felt the need to go back to it, I just could not seem to get myself to switch to my VSCodium instance with all of its code open. Maybe it's burnout. Maybe it's forgetfulness, hell, maybe it's just lazyness. Whatever it is, it gets pretty aggravating.

I get bored or uninterested pretty quick and easily if I wasn't already. I don't know if bored and uninterested are necessarily the way I would put it, but once I leave something, I often find it hard to come back. I guess you could call that intertia. I end up procrastinating a lot and add personally interesting stuff to my to-do lists but barely end up actually starting stuff until it's really needed. I guess a somewhat positive side-effect to this is that I get plenty of time to think of an approach I like. This doesn't just affect my development though, it generally affects my whole life. It's not that I don't do my homework or assignments, but that I'll put them off way until they really need to start getting done and I end up having to cram or rush stuff and generally feel dissatisfied with the work. It takes a lot of willpower.

I've heard working out can really help with this, but like before, my intertia interferes with this. I have a quite a few friends who actively work out and they have told me that I should join them but it's just hard. I'm not overweight or really need the extra gain. I'm about 6' and slightly below average weight for my age. I'm not medically diagnosed with ADD or ADHD, but I would think that I've generally shown characteristics towards the ADD side which would help explain a lot of this.

I don't know, my life at this point is just kind of messy. Me actively writing this post and creating a blog section for this website is part of my procrastination currently. I'll end it here and try to get some stuff done.
